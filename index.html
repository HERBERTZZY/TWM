Abstract

Generative modeling has emerged as a mainstream paradigm for data synthesis, offering a promising solution to data scarcity and insufficient coverage of long-tail scenarios. Despite remarkable progresses in autonomous driving, its potential for traffic data generation from roadside or infrastructure perspective, remains largely underexplored. To this end, we propose the first traffic-oriented world model termed Traffic World Model (TWM), for controllable and high-fidelity multimodal data generation. It is built upon a unified conditional Diffusion Transformer (cDiT) architecture, which generates controllable roadside-view image from structured road-agent layout and then utilizes as the common visual foundation for subsequent multimodal synthesis. TWM jointly supports layout-to-image generation, image-to-video generation, safety-critical event generation, and image-to-LiDAR point-cloud generation, enabling controllable modeling of temporal evolution, traffic participant relationship, geometric topology within a coherent manner. Extensive experiments on several publicly-available and private datasets demonstrates TWM achieves the state-of-the-art performance in generation quality, controllability, and multimodal scalability among four tasks. We hope this work would serve as a scalable multimodal traffic scenario generation engine, and the code is open-sourced soon.

Pipeline

Overview of the proposed Traffic World Model (TWM), a traffic-view multimodal data generation framework. Starting from textual traffic descriptions and structural road priors, the framework first synthesizes a controllable roadside-view image through a conditional Diffusion Transformer (cDiT) backbone. The generated image then serves as a shared scene anchor for subsequent multimodal generation tasks, including traffic-video synthesis, safety-critical event generation, depth prediction, and LiDAR generation.

Visualization

Qualitative results on image generation. Generated images across different road segments and weather conditions. The road layout and vehicle pose remain spatially consistent, while weather, illumination and atmospheric appearance are controllably varied.

Qualitative results on video generation. Generated driving videos conditioned on different reference frames. The outputs preserve the visual characteristics of real camera footage, including realistic exposure, motion blur and sensor-like texture, rather than exhibiting an overly sharp or animation-like appearance. In rainy scenes, the generated videos further reproduce plausible water spray and mist effects.

Qualitative results on event generation. The top row shows the original images, and the bottom row presents the corresponding edited results with inserted objects. The inserted objects (highlighted by red rectangle) are naturally integrated into the scene with realistic geometry, scale, lighting and occlusion, demonstrating support for a wide range of vehicles and roadside objects.

Qualitative results on LiDAR generation. The generated point clouds capture realistic spatial structures and fine-grained geometric details, including road boundaries, vehicles and surrounding scene elements.
